# Architecture Decision Record Template

## Purpose

An Architecture Decision Record, also known as an ADR, is a lightweight document used to capture important technical decisions.

The goal is to make decisions visible, understandable, and traceable over time.

## Title

Short name of the decision.

Example:

```text
ADR-001: Use a human-in-the-loop approach for AI-generated email responses
```

## Status

Choose one:

```text
Proposed | Accepted | Deprecated | Replaced
```

## Context

Describe the problem, business need, constraints, and relevant technical background.

Useful questions:

- What problem are we trying to solve?
- What business goal is related to this decision?
- What constraints exist?
- What risks should be considered?
- What technical context is important?

## Decision

Describe the decision that was made.

The decision should be clear, specific, and easy to understand.

## Rationale

Explain why this decision was selected.

Include the reasoning, criteria, and trade-offs behind the decision.

## Alternatives Considered

List other options that were evaluated.

For each alternative, briefly explain why it was accepted or rejected.

Example:

```text
Alternative 1: Fully automated workflow
Alternative 2: Manual workflow
Alternative 3: Human-in-the-loop workflow
```

## Consequences

Describe the expected impact of the decision.

### Positive Consequences

- Benefit 1
- Benefit 2
- Benefit 3

### Negative Consequences

- Trade-off 1
- Trade-off 2
- Trade-off 3

## Risks

Identify risks related to this decision.

Examples:

- Security risk
- Privacy risk
- Operational risk
- Maintainability risk
- Adoption risk

## Follow-up Actions

List actions required after the decision.

- Action 1
- Action 2
- Action 3

## Related Documentation

Add links to related documents, diagrams, issues, or repositories.
